MICRO ALIENS?



We expect the danger from outer space, in general, from high-tech aliens and colliding meteorites. However, after the last covid19 pandemic, we have seen that we need to be on our guard against dangerous germs, viruses and bacteria that may come with meteorites. If we can send microbes with the probes we send to space, our world can be infected with diseases that may be a global threat after coming from space, by air or wind.

Don’t say it’s impossible. It could be. After all, didn’t life on earth come from outer space? While there are many creatures that can live at high temperatures and low temperatures even in our world, living things that have evolved according to the conditions of their own planets, especially at the level of microorganisms, can come to our world and multiply rapidly in ideal living conditions here and pose a global threat.

If the ancestors of today’s living things came to our planet from elsewhere in the universe and started life many years ago, this is likely to happen again. It’s happened before, it could probably happen again.

Life can exist anywhere in the universe. It doesn’t have to be an advanced civilization. extraterrestrials may not come here. But they may also have germs, viruses or various kinds of infectious diseases. Although living things themselves cannot come, microorganisms that are thrown into space by close earth-body collisions, the severe gravity of mutual satellites or severe storms under low gravity can easily reproduce in suitable climate conditions on the satellites or planets they can reach. Of course, the most suitable climate conditions that we know in the universe exist in our world.

When we look at the living things in the world, especially humans and animals breathe through the lungs or trachea, which is suitable for the living conditions of our planet. They can live between certain temperature values, which are not very flexible. But smaller, bacterial microorganisms can survive in harsher climatic conditions at lower or higher temperatures. Therefore, it seems possible that such creatures, which have evolved to be even more durable in harsh space conditions, especially in the climate of their own planets, will survive and travel to planets or interstellar space without serious damage.

For now, these predictions don’t make much sense, but even if there is a very small probability, yes, they can happen. It’s happened in the past. Why not again?
