MAGELLAN PROJECT



ARTIFICIAL HABITATS



Magellan Project

Is there life other than us in the universe? This question is one of the most curious questions of mankind throughout history. Especially in the last century, with the increasing scientific developments, more intensive researches have started to search for the answer to this question. Billions of dollars are being made to find this answer, and these investments seem to continue in the future.

It is not known whether we can find the answer we are looking for in these studies. Even if we find it, can we go there in the vast expanse of the universe? Even if we can’t go, can we have a dialogue with the life forms there? We will see this together in the future.



WHILE LOOKING FOR THE ANSWER TO THIS QUESTION. CAN WE ASK OURSELVES THE FOLLOWING QUESTION ?



“I wonder if we can establish a life environment on other planets?” Why not?

We will eventually even send humans to other planets, while we are careful not to send germs even with probes yet. What about the places we can’t set foot? If we take ourselves along with our microbes, we can take the species we want to space travel in the quantities we want.

The plants and animals we send to a planet or satellite that we think may be suitable for life will evolve according to the environment they are in, and will try to adapt the environment and create its habitat. Although this process will take many years, a sustainable life will emerge at the middle point. Isn’t that how life on earth came about anyway?

All living things on earth can only live in the climatic conditions of this planet. All atmospheric features seem to have been specially designed for living things on the planet. Or, living things have adapted to these atmospheric conditions.

What about us? Can we provide this design on other moons or planets? Difficult but not impossible.





MAGELLAN PROJECT



First, we need to find a celestial body that we think might be suitable for life. Here we can leave creatures that have previously been genetically modified in the laboratory environment or can live in difficult conditions. For example, mosses, tardigrades. After these creatures start respiration and photosynthesis in the environments they are left in, they will begin to release substances such as oxygen, carbon dioxide or methane into the environment. These substances will provide mutual benefits for living things to survive. While these are being provided, they will start to keep up with the environment physically. When the food chain is also provided, it means that a sustainable climate and habitat have emerged there.

This theory can be tested experimentally in different regions of the earth, such as the desert, the poles, or the climatic conditions of any planet.

It may have been thought of before. It may even have been tried. No information available. I believe it will be tested both on earth and on any planet or moon in the universe because it is easy and cost effective to implement.

Who knows, maybe the creatures that evolved in these artificial habitats in the future will establish an advanced civilization millions of years later. Who knows?
